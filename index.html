About Me

I am a PhD Research Scholar in the broad domain of Entomology with a focus on the ecology and taxonomy of insects. I completed my Master’s degree in Zoology from Gauhati University, Assam, and I am currently pursuing my PhD at Rabindranath Tagore University, Assam.

My doctoral research investigates the ecological roles of termites across different landscapes of Assam, with particular emphasis on their functional contributions to forest and non-forest ecosystems.

Research Interests

  • Insect Ecology
  • Insect Taxonomy and Systematics
  • Termite Diversity and Functional Ecology
  • Ecosystem Processes and Nutrient Cycling
  • Landscape-level Biodiversity Patterns

Current PhD Research

Title (Working): Ecological Roles of Termites in Different Landscapes of Assam

My PhD research focuses on understanding how termite communities vary across forest and non-forest landscapes and how their functional roles influence soil properties, decomposition processes, and ecosystem stability. The study integrates systematic field sampling, ecological analysis, and biodiversity assessment.
